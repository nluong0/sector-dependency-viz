Sector Dependency by County

The USDA Economic Research Search (ERS) has codified “six mutually exclusive categories of economic dependence and six overlapping categories of policy-relevant themes” for each county in the United States. Economic dependence types include “farming, mining, manufacturing, Federal/State government, recreation, and non-specialized counties”. Below, you'll be able to explore which U.S. counties were dependent on which sectors in 2015 as well as how the socioeconomic variables for counties in each sector-depenency group interact.

Definitions

A manufacturing-dependent county is one where manufacturing accounted for 23% or more of the county's earnings or 16% of the employment averaged over 2010-12.

A farming-dependent county is one where farming accounted for at 25% or more of the county's earnings or 16% or more of the employment averaged over 2010-2012.

A mining-dependent county is one where mining accounted for 13% or more of the county's earnings or 8% of the employment averaged over 2010-12.

A recreation-dependent county is determined by percentage of wage and salary employment in entertainment and recreation, accommodations, eating and drinking places, and real estate as a percentage of all employment reported by the Bureau of Economic Analysis; percentage of total personal income reported for these same categories by the Bureau of Economic Analysis; and percentage of vacant housing units intended for seasonal or occasional use reported in the 2010 Census.

A nonspecialized county is one does not fall into any of the above categories.
