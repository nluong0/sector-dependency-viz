Sector Dependency by County

The USDA Economic Research Search (ERS) has codified “six mutually exclusive categories of economic dependence and six overlapping categories of policy-relevant themes” for each county in the United States. Economic dependence types include “farming, mining, manufacturing, Federal/State government, recreation, and non-specialized counties”. Policy-relevant types include “low education, low employment, persistent poverty, persistent child poverty, population loss, and retirement destination.” An area's economic dependence can heavily shape its development, need for various types of policy programs or interventions, and business climate. Inspecting what socioeconomic and demographic factors correlate with the various types of economic dependence can suggest narratives for how those economic dependence came about. Pinpointing the main factors associated with these dependencies help draw relationships between them as well.
